About Me

My name is Donna Breier, and I have spent over 25 years doing what I love most — helping children learn, grow, and believe in themselves. I began my career as a classroom teacher in Illinois public and private schools, working with students from kindergarten through 8th grade across a wide range of learning styles and abilities. Since 2019, I have been offering one-on-one private tutoring from my home classroom in Sleepy Hollow, IL.

Every child learns differently. Some need a slower pace. Some need a different explanation. Some just need someone who truly believes in them. My approach is rooted in patience, encouragement, and a genuine love of teaching. I take the time to understand how each student thinks and what they need — then I meet them exactly where they are.

I have a deep familiarity with Catholic school curricula and the academic and behavioral expectations that come with them. Having taught and tutored extensively in Catholic school settings throughout the Northwest Suburbs, I understand the standards these schools hold — and I know how to help students rise to meet them.

I hold a Master's degree in Teacher Leadership from North Park University and a Bachelor's degree in Elementary Education from Purdue University. I am Illinois-certified through grade 9, with a special endorsement in Reading, and I am trained in the UFLI Foundations reading program — one of the most effective structured literacy approaches available today.

What I Offer

📚

Trained in the UFLI Foundations Reading Program

UFLI Foundations is a structured, systematic phonics program developed by the University of Florida Literacy Institute. It is one of the most research-backed approaches to teaching reading available — designed to help struggling readers build a strong foundation in phonics, decoding, and fluency from the very beginning. I bring this proven method directly into every reading session.

📖

Reading

Reading is the foundation of all learning, and it's my specialty. Using the UFLI Foundations program, I teach students to decode words, build fluency, and truly comprehend what they read. I assess and target all four components of reading fluency:

  • Comprehension
  • Accuracy
  • Speed
  • Expression
➕

Math

Math doesn't have to be frustrating. I break down concepts step by step, using methods that make sense to each individual student. From basic number sense in kindergarten to pre-algebra in middle school, I help students build real understanding — not just memorization.

✏️

Writing

Strong writing starts with strong thinking. Using the Writing Workshop approach, I help students develop their voice, organize their ideas, and express themselves clearly and confidently — from simple sentences all the way to multi-paragraph essays.

🎯

Study Skills

Many students struggle not because they don't understand the material, but because they don't know how to study. I teach organization, time management, test preparation strategies, and note-taking skills that serve students for the rest of their academic lives.

📝

English & Grammar

A strong grasp of grammar is the backbone of clear communication. Using Voyages in English by Loyola Press — a trusted resource widely used in Catholic schools — I teach the fundamentals of English grammar in a structured, accessible way. From parts of speech and sentence structure to punctuation and usage, I work with struggling students to build the foundational skills they need to write and speak with confidence.

  • Every session is fully individualized — no cookie-cutter curriculum
  • Builds academic confidence alongside subject skills
  • Flexible scheduling to fit your family's routine
  • Ongoing communication with parents about progress and goals

Common Questions

Here are a few of the questions I hear most often from parents. Every family's situation is a little different — so if you don't see your question here, please don't hesitate to reach out. I'm always happy to talk.

  • What grades do you tutor? I work with students from Kindergarten through 8th grade. Whether your child is just starting to learn to read or is heading into middle school and struggling to keep up, I can help.
  • What subjects do you cover? My primary focus areas are reading, math, writing, and study skills. Reading is my specialty — particularly for students who are behind or struggling with phonics and fluency. I also support students in general homework help and academic organization.
  • Where do sessions take place? All sessions are held at my home classroom in Sleepy Hollow, IL. It's a quiet, dedicated learning space — comfortable for kids and completely free of distractions.
  • Do you work with students who have IEPs or learning differences? Absolutely. Throughout my career I have worked with many students with IEP plans, learning differences, and diverse academic needs. I know how to adapt my approach to meet each student where they are and help them make real progress.
  • How do I get started? Simply call or email me. We'll have a quick conversation about your child, and if it feels like a good fit, we'll schedule a free consultation to talk things through in more detail. From there, we'll set up a regular schedule and get to work.

Still have questions? I'd love to hear from you. Every child is different — let's talk about yours.
